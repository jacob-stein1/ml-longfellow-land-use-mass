Here is our database of racial restrictive covenants. Combing through the files of the King County Recorder's Office, we have uncovered 416 deeds and covenants containing racial restrictions that apply to scores of neighborhoods in Seattle and its suburbs. This is just a sample of the restrictions that once shaped the housing options for King County residents. We have examined only about one quarter of the deeds that were filed during the years between 1927 and 1948 when most racial restrictive covenants were written into deeds.

The database is arranged by regions, starting with Seattle neighborhoods, then Eastside suburbs, North King County, South King County, Islands, and finally a separate section of cemetaries. Click on the icon in column 4 to see a pdf copy of many of the actual deeds.

University of Washington students enrolled in History 353 researched this database. Carolyn Lind is section coordinator and located more than half of these deeds. She was joined by Karen Oliver, Pauline Chardoul-Sutter, Susan Frost, Dien Luu, Doug Marcoux, BreeAnn Larios, Marco Vargas, Rebecca Gray, Tracie Newbins, Amber Price, Nichole Murray, and Jonathan Brooks. Patrick Kelly and Brittany Barrett digitized the documents.
